The Enlightenment

Introduction

The Enlightenment, or the Age of Reason, was a transformative intellectual and philosophical movement in 17th and 18th-century Europe. It championed reason, science, and individualism.

Key Ideas

Enlightenment thinkers advocated for human rights, democracy, and the separation of church and state. Notable figures include John Locke, Voltaire, and Jean-Jacques Rousseau.

Impact

The Enlightenment shaped modern democratic societies, inspiring revolutions and challenging traditional authority.
