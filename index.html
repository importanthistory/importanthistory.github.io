The Enlightenment

Introduction

The Enlightenment, also known as the Age of Reason, was an intellectual and philosophical movement that dominated Europe during the 17th and 18th centuries. It emphasized reason, science, individualism, and skepticism of traditional authority.

Key Ideas

Enlightenment thinkers promoted ideas such as human rights, democracy, and the separation of church and state. Notable figures include John Locke, Voltaire, and Jean-Jacques Rousseau.

Impact

The Enlightenment laid the groundwork for significant social and political changes, influencing revolutions and the development of modern democratic societies.
